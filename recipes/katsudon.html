Katsudon

Ingredients

  • Boneless Pork Chops
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Soy Sauce
  • Flour
  • 1 cup panko
  • 1/2 cup dashi stock or chicken stock
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons mirin
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 medium onion
  • 2 servings steamed white rice
    •

    Procedure

    1. Season the pounded pork chops with salt and pepper.

    2. Dust with a light, even coating of flour.

    3. In one shallow bowl, beat 1 of the eggs. Put the panko into another shallow bowl.

    4. Add a thin, even layer of oil to a cast-iron pan or skillet over medium heat. The oil is ready when you drop a panko breadcrumb into it and it sizzles.

    5. Dip the flour-dusted pork into the egg to coat both sides.

    6. Transfer the pork to the panko and press it evenly into the meat to get a good coating.

    7. Carefully lay the pork chops in the hot oil and cook for 5 to 6 minutes on one side, until golden brown.

    8. Flip and cook the other side for another 5 to 6 minutes, or until browned, crispy, and cooked through.

    9. Put the dashi soup stock in a pan and heat on medium heat.

    10. Add the soy sauce, mirin, and sugar to the soup and bring to a boil. Remove from the heat.

    11. To cook 1 serving of katsudon, put 1/4 of the soup and 1/4 of the sliced onion in a small skillet. Simmer for a few minutes on medium heat.

    12. Add 1 serving of tonkatsu pieces (half of 1 pork cutlet) to the pan and simmer on low heat for a few minutes.

    13. Beat another one of the eggs in a bowl. Bring the soup to a boil and pour the egg over the tonkatsu and onion.

    14. Turn the heat down to low and cover with a lid. Cook until the egg has set and remove it from the heat. The egg should be cooked through.

    15. Serve by placing 1 serving of steamed rice in a large rice bowl. Top with the simmered tonkatsu on top of the rice. Repeat to make 3 more servings.
