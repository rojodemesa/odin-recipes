Takoyaki

Batter Ingredients

  • 200g flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 450ml water
  • Pinch of Dashi Stock Powder
    •

    Fillings Ingredients

  • 100g chopped fresh boiled octopus
  • 1 bunch chopped spring onion
  • red pickled ginger
  • tempura flakes
    •

    Toppings Ingredients

  • takoyaki sauce
  • japanese mayonnaise
  • aosa powdered seaweed
  • katsuobushi bonito flakes
    •

    Procedure

    1. Start by creating the batter. Grabe a large bowl and mix together 2 eggs, 200g of flour, 450ml of water, and a little dashi stock. Set this aside. If you have the ready-made okonomiyaki flour, follow the directions of making the batter and you're good to go.

    2. Place your takoyaki plate on the gas stove on medium heat and heat up a small amount of oil in each hole

    3. Cut up your octopus in smaller pieces. Place a piece of octopus in each of the semi-circular holes, and then fill up each hole to the top with batter mix. You can even overflow the batter out of the hole to make it easier to flip them later.

    4. Add chopped spring onion, red pickled ginger, and tempura flakes to each hole

    5. Once the takoyaki are about half cooked, flip them over. Use a small wooden skewer to poke the outside of the batter and flip it over within the hole

    6. Keep rotating them until golden brown

    7. Place the takoyaki on a plate and smother them with takoyaki sauce and japanese mayonnaise. Then sprinkle a bit of powdered seaweed and bonito flakes on top and enjoy
